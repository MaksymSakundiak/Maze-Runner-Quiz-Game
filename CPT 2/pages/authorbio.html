About James Dashner

James Dashner is an American writer known for his speculative fiction. He is the author of the Maze Runner series, which has been a major hit among young adult readers and has been adapted into a successful film series.

Born on November 26, 1972, in Austell, Georgia, Dashner developed a passion for writing early in life. He graduated from Brigham Young University with a degree in accounting but soon turned to writing full-time. His works often explore dystopian themes, blending action, mystery, and complex characters.

The Maze Runner series, his most notable work, includes titles like "The Maze Runner," "The Scorch Trials," and "The Death Cure." These books have captivated millions of readers around the world and continue to be a favorite among fans of the genre.
